About Wonder

发表于 2022-02-09
本文字数： 4.2k 阅读时长 ≈ 4 分钟

Foreword

During this winter holiday, I read the book Wonder on the recommendation of my English teacher. And, of course, it’s always impossible that you won’t have to do anything after your teacher assigns you such a HUGE&HARD task. Thus, here is my work.

Overview

At the beginning of the book, we’re introduced to a boy who does the ordinary thing but owns an not that ordinary face. That is because several operations had been taken on him at his birth. As a result, the poor boy, August Pullman (Auggie) has a facial deformity.

Here’s how the story happens: In order to get on with other children, Auggie’s parents made the decision to send him to a public school. Before the term starts, he was taken to the principal of the school and introduced to some kids in his grade. There, he met his first friend, Jack Will.

At school, though Auggie truly made a few friends, he was still teased by some naughty boys because of his special appearance. During this period of time, Auggie always believes that Jack is his true friend. Until-

On Halloween, wearing a costume, Auggie overheard that Jack said to others with laughter that if he looked like Auggie, he would kill himself. Auggie is heatbroken. However, Jack is unaware of this.

Then the story switches to Via, Auggie’s elder sister. Since her younger brother needs their parents care more indeed, Via didn’t get much attention from her parents and was often pushed aside in favor of his brother. Grans was the only person who put her first before she died. Misfortunes never come singly. Via also met some problems in her social life.

She no longer has any friends and her mother didn’t really even care about her. She became a loner at school.

On Halloween, she discovered that Auggie came back earlier than ever and refused to go trick or treating with her. He revealed that Jack had said something about him and made him feel painful. Via convinced him that he must move past such dilemmas so that bullies won’t affect his future happiness and success at school because there will always be mean people around even if you don’t want them there; They just simply exist like germs floating around us all the time waiting to infect someone else’s existance when given an opportunity through our own actions towards them.

On the other side, Summer became Auggie’s new friend anyway. Summer knew that it was Jack who had said something mean about Auggie at school, which hurt Auggie greatly. So she told Jack what he had done and left him a clue: ‘Bleeding Scream.’

Next section stands by Jack. He remembered when Mr. Tushman asked him to be friends with Auggie and he was amazed when Auggie showed him differently from other rich students at the private school. Of course, Jack couldn’t forget how Auggie helped him at the science test. He is sorry that he caught up in the social dynamics of his grade. From the deepest of his heart, he actually wanted to make friends with Auggie. But Julian tried to persuade him that it’s not worth it. This got Jack really angry so he punches Julian in the face. After a series of matters, Jack and Auggie finally got back as friends in winter. But when they got back to school, you can tell the hostility from Julian and a few classmates.

The story then switches to Via’s boyfriend, Justin. He is amazed at how perfect the Pullman family seems in comparison to his broken-up family. Meanwhile, he and Miranda got the lead role of their school play, Our Town. Miranda felt guilty about Via so she let Via be the leader role. And Via did really a great job.

In the end, Auggie was welcomed by his classmates. Auggie’s fifth grade year culminates in victory, and he is admired by students and teachers alike for his courage, his perseverance in the face of difficulty, and the quiet strength of his character.

My Feelings

I was deeply touched after reading this novel. The courage Auggie had, the tortuous experience between Auggie and Jack Will, the ignorance and sacrifice of Via in her family for her brother… These are all very precious treasure between people.

No one would disagree that Wonder is a wonderful book. It uses simple, pure and positive values to teach everyone how to love others, to treat others nicely, to tolerate the unfair of the world, to stand by your side and to persist on your heart. What the book touches is every heart that has been misunderstood and hurt.

Fortunately, when he grows up, Auggie’s parents and sister have always been his strong backings. With their love, care and support, Auggie inspired many people around him with his courage, kindness and intelligence, and gained friendship,respect and love. Finally, Auggie grew into an incredible ‘Wonder’ in everyone’s mind.

As a family theme novel, Wonder tries to teach people how to correctly understand family affection, friendship and love, how to maintain authenticity and original heart in a utilitarian society, and how to love people around us better.

Just like what Auggie said,

‘Everyone in the world should get a standing ovation at least once in their life because we all overcometh the world. ‘
